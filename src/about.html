We built Aspen because small jails deserve better.

Aspen was built for the county jails that the healthcare industry has largely ignored. Facilities under 120 beds with no dedicated medical team, officers making healthcare decisions they weren’t trained for, and counties carrying far more liability than they need to.

We built it because we kept seeing that gap with smaller county jails and nobody was filling it.

The team behind Aspen comes from Ivy Correctional Medicine, a corrections medical provider with 15 years of hands-on experience inside county jails. This experience now powers Aspen, making corrections-specific telehealth available to smaller facilities, staffed exclusively by clinicians who’ve worked in jail environments, not adapted from community providers.

It shows up in our:

  • Clinical protocols and staffing standards built for corrections
  • Understanding of how inmates present and how manipulation operates
  • Prescribing practices calibrated for a custody environment
  • Medical care that integrates into secure operations without disrupting facility flow

Our mission is simple: protect inmates, lower county liability, reduce costly medical transports, and make sure that even the smallest facilities can access professional, corrections-appropriate healthcare.

With Aspen, jails get:

  • Daily nursing sessions for general and chronic care needs
  • Weekly provider visits for diagnosis, treatment, and medication management
  • Weekly mental health sessions for assessment and support
  • 24/7 access to nursing and prescriber support for urgent needs
  • Full administrative ownership of medical tasks, freeing deputies to focus on security

When Brian Egeland, Jail Captain at Rosebud County in Montana, called us after his jail started with Aspen, he said it “felt like a weight had been lifted.” This is why we built this.
