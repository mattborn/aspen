Why Small Jails Get Left Behind

If you run a jail with fewer than 80 beds, you already know this: most healthcare vendors won’t even return your call—let alone solve your problems.

Cost Prohibitive

On-site care is often too expensive and you usually don’t need a full time nurse.

Overlooked by Big Vendors

Large vendors prioritize the big facilities. Smaller jails just aren’t their priority.

Non-Corrections Clinicians

Community providers are frequently manipulated by inmates and prescribe unnecessary medications and treatments, generating burden for officers and extra costs.

Patchwork Systems

If you don’t have medical, your staff ends up triaging inmates and coordinating medical care on their own.

Without the right medical team, the facility carries the burden.

Overloaded Officers

Officers get pulled off their core duties to manage transports, sit at ERs, and track down prescriptions.

Increased Risk

Your people are making triage decisions without medical training — and if something goes wrong, the liability is yours.

Con Games

Inmates manipulate weaknesses in the system — gaming their way into transport or meds they don’t need.

Community doctors often prescribe medications and treatments that aren’t necessary in a jail setting. That leads to more pills, more cheeking, more risk, and more time spent on med pass.

Administrative Burden
