Why Small Jails Get Left Behind

You're not just short on medical care. You're stuck with a system that was never built for you.

Cost Prohibitive

On-site care is expensive and rarely justified at your population size. You don't need a full-time nurse — but you do need reliable care.

Overlooked by Big Vendors

Large medical contractors focus on high-volume, high-revenue facilities. Smaller jails just aren't their priority.

Under-qualified Local Help

Local providers may not have correctional experience — and that leads to poor prescribing, gaps in care, and unnecessary transports.

Patchwork Systems

If you don't have coverage, your staff ends up triaging inmates and coordinating medical care on their own.

And When That System Fails, It Falls on Your Team

Overloaded Officers

Officers get pulled off their core duties to manage transports, sit at ERs, and track down prescriptions.

Increased Risk

Staff without clinical training are forced to make triage decisions — decisions that carry real legal risk.

Con Games

Inmates manipulate gaps in the system — gaming their way into transport or meds they don't need.

Administrative Burden

Your team is doing medical admin on top of security, intake, and everything else.

Dismissed at the ER

Hospital staff hear so many false complaints from inmates that they stop listening.

Too Many Meds, Too Much Risk

Community doctors often prescribe medications that aren't necessary in a jail setting.
